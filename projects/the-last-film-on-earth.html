the last film on earth

2024 · 15 min · english · experimental / documentary / nature
In a world where cinema has become obsolete, a lone filmmaker embarks on a journey to capture the last moving images of Earth. Through a blend of found footage, digital artifacts, and intimate observations of nature, this experimental documentary explores the relationship between human memory, technological obsolescence, and the enduring beauty of the natural world. As the filmmaker documents disappearing landscapes and forgotten technologies, the film itself becomes a meditation on the impermanence of both human creation and the planet we inhabit.
Direction, cinematography, editing: Andrés Isaza Giraldo
Sound design: Diana Muñoz
Music: Sara Fernández
Color grading: Mauricio Reyes
Production: Independent production
"Andrés Isaza Giraldo's 'The Last Film on Earth' is a haunting meditation on the intersection of human creativity and environmental consciousness. Through its innovative use of found footage and intimate nature cinematography, the film creates a powerful commentary on our relationship with technology and the natural world. The experimental approach challenges conventional documentary form while remaining deeply accessible and emotionally resonant. This is a work that will stay with viewers long after the credits roll, prompting reflection on our collective responsibility to preserve both our artistic heritage and the planet that inspires it."
Sarah Johnson from Experimental Cinema Quarterly
